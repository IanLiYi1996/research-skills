# Tips for Effective Annotations

1. **Write annotations in your own words** - Don't just copy the abstract
2. **Be critical** - Evaluate strengths and weaknesses
3. **Note methodology** - This helps assess quality and comparability
4. **Highlight relevance** - Make it clear why you included this paper
5. **Include page numbers** - For quotes and specific findings
6. **Use consistent format** - Makes it easier to compare across papers
7. **Update as needed** - Add notes as you gain new insights
8. **Tag effectively** - Use keywords that help you find papers later

---

# Organization Strategies

### By Theme
Group annotations by major themes in your research

### By Methodology
Organize by research design or analytical approach

### By Chronology
Arrange by publication date to show evolution of field

### By Quality
Sort by quality rating to prioritize high-quality studies

### By Relevance
Order by how directly each paper addresses your question

---

# Integration with Your Review

### When Writing Literature Review:
1. Use annotations as starting point for summaries
2. Group papers with similar annotations together
3. Refer to critical evaluations for discussing limitations
4. Use relevance notes to structure your narrative
5. Check tags to ensure comprehensive coverage

### Citation Management:
- Import citations into reference manager (Zotero, Mendeley, EndNote)
- Link annotation file to citation entry
- Export citations in required format for manuscript
- Keep annotations synced with citation database
